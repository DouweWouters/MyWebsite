Project PYB

Project PYB began as a design competition by kampC, a research group for sustainable living solutions. They printed Europe's first two-story concrete house. Winning the competition allowed me to print my design, the modular sitting bench called ZITBOOG, on their 3D concrete printer.

This led to Project PYB (Print Your Bench), simplifying the entire process from customization to manufacturing. An interface enables users to select parameters, which are then converted into a code for a robot arm to print the bench according to the customer's specifications.

Watchtower

Watchtower originated as a project during my time at the Polytech University in St. Petersburg, Russia, during a winter school program. The task was to create a prototype within a two-week timeframe. Faced with the issue of my watch getting scratched while charging on the ground, I devised a solution: a docking station.

However, I wanted it to be more than just functional; I aimed to make it visually appealing. Inspired by the clock-topped towers of churches, I designed the Watchtower—a docking station shaped like a church tower—for my watch.

Stone dumping vessel

At MULTI Engineering, for my bachelor thesis, I designed and analysed a modular stone dumping vessel. It's used to push rocks off the deck into the water for reinforcing dikes and covering underwater cables.

The challenge was to create a design that could handle 300 tons of stone on each side, without using bolts or screws due to saltwater exposure. The entire vessel had to be crane-mounted for easy assembly and disassembly. Detailed analysis was conducted to ensure the hydraulic pistons and structure could withstand the required forces.

Gen Z-bike

Our team, consisting of three students including myself, was tasked with designing a mountain bike frame using CFRP (Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer). The unique requirement was to shape the frame in the form of the letter Z.

Our project involved designing molds for manufacturing, as well as creating detailed documentation on the materials and carbon fiber layup process for the molds. Unfortunately, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, we were unable to fabricate the frame or the molds as the school was closed during that period. However, we successfully completed the full design of the Z-frame using the freeform feature in Autodesk Inventor.

RC Eco Shell

The Eco Shell competition provides a platform for participants to design and construct eco-friendly, sustainable cars, fostering innovation. Our school, Thomas More UAS, achieved victory in this competition, leading to the creation of a smaller RC version of the winning car.

As a team of three students, our project entailed redesigning the body and drivetrain of the RC car, incorporating captivating features such as lights. This experience marked a pivotal milestone in my academic journey, fuelling my passion for engineering and automotive design.
